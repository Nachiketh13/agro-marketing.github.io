ABOUT SUGARCANE

Sugarcane is a giant tropical grass from the family Graminaceae, whose stalk has the particular capacity to store a crystallizable sugar, sucrose. Its main use is in industrial processing of the stalks to make rum.Sugarcane provides a juice, which is used for making white sugar, and jaggery (gur) and many by-products 1ike bagasse and molasses. Bagasse is used as a fuel, for production of fiber board, papers, plastics and furfural.Brazil is the largest producer of sugarcane in the world. India is the second largest producer of sugarcane. These two countries will respectively account for about 21% and 18% of the world's total sugar output by 2030. With this, India has emerged as the world's largest producer and consumer of sugar as well as the world's 2nd largest exporter of sugar.
