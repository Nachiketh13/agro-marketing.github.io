HOW TO Grow Rice

Plant the rice seeds throughout the soil, during the fall or spring season. Get rid of the weeds, till the beds, and level the soil. If you are using buckets, fill them with at least 6 inches (15 cm) of moist soil. Then add the rice seeds.Preparing the land. Farmers get to work on the fields and will try to finish all the preparations before the rainy season. ... 2 - Transplanting the paddy seedlings.
3 - Maintenance of the field.
4 - Harvesting.
Rice plants grow to a height of three to four feet over an average of 120 days after planting. During this time, farmers irrigate the rice fields using the method that best fits that field or farm. Nitrogen Excess. Nitrogen application in sufficient amount helps rice grow faster and provide us with high-quality grains. However, many farmers apply a larger amount than the one that rice needs. Excessive nitrogen use can lead to rich growth which attracts a lot of pests.
